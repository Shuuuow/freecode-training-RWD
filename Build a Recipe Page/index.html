Falafle Recipe

Is a main stay dish, in Arabic cuisine. It is a vegetarian dish made of Hummus and served as a sandwich or eaten simply as is.

Ingredients

  • 400 grams canned chickpeas (drained and rinsed, or use dried chickpeas soaked overnight)
  • 70 grams fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 30 grams flour (all-purpose or chickpea flour)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Blend the mixture. Add chickpeas, parsley, onion, and garlic to a food processor. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs—it should hold together but still be slightly crumbly.
  2. Add the dry ingredients. Mix in flour, cumin, coriander, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Pulse again until just combined.
  3. Chill. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 30 minutes (this helps the falafel hold together better).
  4. Shape. Form the mixture into balls about the size of a walnut or use two spoons to create oval shapes.
  5. Fry. Heat oil to 175°C in a deep pan or deep fryer. Fry the falafel for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown. Don't overcrowd the pan.
  6. Drain. Place on paper towels to remove excess oil.
